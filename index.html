About Apollo Foundation

Apollo Foundation is a non-profit organization, composed of passionate students and several well-achieved regional directors, that work to provide better and more enhanced education to underprivileged students around the world. Along with our dedicated volunteers, we have partnered with the Asante Africa Foundation, the Taleem Doo Foundation, and Area9 Lyceum to provide such education to impoverished students around the world. Furthermore, the Apollo Foundation has commenced various Initiatives, namely the India Initiative, Middle East Initiative, the Cameroon Initiative, and the East Africa Initiative. Within these, we help schools by forming projects to provide funds as well as curriculum-based tutorial videos and worksheets. Some of our big projects within these initiatives include the Roshni Project, in which we help underprivileged students in the Roshni Learning Center in Bihar, India, as well as helping impoverished schools in Cameroon.

Requirements and eligibility

Register for your specific category: Sub-junior: 10-13 years old, or Junior: 14-18 years old.

Must sign up with a discord account and join our server for communications: https://discord.gg/dcw7Uyu

List the email addresses of all members on your team for information and updates

Stay respectful, open-minded and tolerant of all people regardless of their race, gender, or age, as this is an international event

Hackathon teams will be a maximum of FOUR people.

Teams should be made up exclusively of students who are not organizers, those affiliated with judges, and the management team.

All team members should be present at the event. Leaving the venue for some time to hack elsewhere is fine.

All work on a project should be done during the hackathon. Teams CANNOT use prior code or additional resources to gain an upper hand against competitors (execptions: Stack Overflow, APIs and libraries, other frameworks).

A project must consist of a research/task documentation including all the technical and business aspects, as well as the actual project. The documentation must be submitted by adding it to the Apollo Foundation Google Drive. If the actual project is code-based, it must be submitted through GitHub, otherwise, it should be added to Google Drive.

Teams must stop hacking and submit their project once the time is up.

Teams can be disqualified from the competition at the organizers’ discretion. Reasons are limited to breaking the Competition Rules and behaving in an unsporting manner.
